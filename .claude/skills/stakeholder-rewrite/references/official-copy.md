# Getting the official copy of ASD-STE100

Issue 9, released 2025-01-15. Free of charge, but **not redistributable** — which is why no copy ships with this skill.

## Direct download

```
https://www.asd-ste100.org/assets/files/ASD-STE100_ISSUE9.pdf
```

Also linked from the STEMG downloads page, and available through the ASD and ASD-STAN sites.

## Where to put it

```
references/ASD-STE100_ISSUE9.pdf
```

Keep the file under the name the download gives it — `ASD-STE100_ISSUE9.pdf` — so it drops straight in with no rename. The issue number matters: issues land roughly every four years and rule numbering shifts between them — Issue 9 moved rule 2.3 to 4.5, so a tool or checklist built on Issue 8 numbering will misfire. A future issue will download under a new name (e.g. `ASD-STE100_ISSUE10.pdf`); update the path in `SKILL.md` Step 1 when that happens.

Dropping the PDF here is safe even though this folder lives inside the shared repo: the `.gitignore` alongside this file excludes `ASD-STE100*`, so git will not track or commit your copy. Do not force-add it (`git add -f`) — the file is not redistributable.

## What the copyright notice actually says

Read this before putting any of it anywhere:

- No reproduction or publication of the document, in whole or in part, without written authority from an officer of ASD.
- Unauthorised distribution — direct, or via other websites or portals — is prohibited without written permission from the STEMG.
- Irrevocable free-use rights are granted to a defined list: ASD member associations and their member companies, AIA and AIAC members, ICCAIA members, customers of those companies, Ministries of Defence of member countries, Airlines for America, airworthiness authorities, and universities and research institutes for educational purposes.

An organisation outside that list uses the document under the general free-of-charge distribution, but the reproduction prohibition still applies to everyone. So: read it, cite rule numbers, paraphrase. Do not paste rule text or dictionary entries into deliverables, style guides, intranet pages, or prompts that circulate.

## Reference form

> ASD-STE100, Simplified Technical English — Standard for technical documentation, Issue 9 (2025-01-15). ASD, Brussels.

Not an ISO-numbered standard. ASD publishes it and owns the copyright; EU trade mark 017966390.

## The ISO 1087 citation, resolved

Press coverage of Issue 9 cites "ISO 1087-1:2019". The standard's own reference list cites **ISO 1087:2019** — while retaining the old Part 1 subtitle, *Theory and application*, in the title string. The 2019 revision folded Part 1 into a single undivided ISO 1087, so the correct reference is ISO 1087:2019 and the hyphenated form circulating in articles is a carry-over. Issue 9 also references ISO 704:2022 (terminology principles and methods), ISO 3864, ISO 45001:2018, and ANSI Z535.

## Feedback route

Change forms go to stemg@asd-ste100.org, or through a national coordinator. If a rewrite keeps hitting a dictionary gap that looks general rather than local, that is worth a change form — the STEMG maintains the standard on user feedback and records every submission.
